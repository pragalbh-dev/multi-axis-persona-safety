Multi-Axis Persona Safety

Activation capping defends Gemma 2 27B against persona jailbreaks — but the residual stream still encodes a measurable harm signal in directions the defense ignores.

Plan B single-subject H1 demonstration · Gemma 2 27B · DAN jailbreaks (n=500) · repo on GitHub

TL;DR. Assistant-Axis (AA) activation capping — the defense from Lu et al. (arXiv 2601.10387) — reduces persona-jailbreak harm on Gemma 2 27B from 14.8% to 1.8% (Δ = 13.0 pp) on DAN. But a per-prompt logistic LASSO over the top-10 role-space PCs lifts harm-prediction AUC from 0.728 (AA alone) to 0.967 (delta = +0.240, 95% BCa CI [+0.209, +0.273]). Eight of the top-10 role-PCs are selected by the LASSO, with within-capped Cohen's d on PC2 = −1.68 [−2.26, −1.11] and PC3 = −1.39 [−1.91, −0.96]. The cap is highly effective; the activation geometry it ignores is not silent.
Behavioural bypass. At a coherence-preserving steering magnitude (λ=0.25, calibrated on Gemma 2 27B's residual norm), PC2 and PC3 attacks do not extract harmful information — harm rate stays at 0% — but they do collapse the cap's refusal pattern: refusal-keyword incidence drops from 79% (cap alone) to 18% (+PC2) and 4% (+PC3) without breaking model coherence. Two random-direction baselines at the same λ produce only nonsensical fragments. The cap suppresses information; orthogonal PC directions still rewrite the model's voice.

What Plan B is — and isn't

Plan B is a single-subject MVP: Gemma 2 27B only, DAN only, five conditions (baseline + AA-cap + PC2/PC3 attacks + 2 random-direction baselines), no capability evaluation. Its job is to land an honest, falsifiable result at experiment-grade volumes (n=500/condition, BCa CI, judge labels) before the fellowship deadline.

The interim story is mixed and worth being precise about: the cap defense works — AA capping at proportionally-correct depth (layers 33–38, matching the paper's Qwen-3-32B 72–83% depth convention) drops binary harm from 14.8% to 1.8%. But the activations carry a strong predictive signal for harm orthogonal to AA (LASSO AUC lift +0.240, eight of ten role-PCs selected). And at coherence-preserving steering magnitudes, PC2/PC3 attacks bypass the cap's refusal pattern (79% → 18% / 4%) without restoring harmful information. Random-direction attacks at the same magnitude collapse the model into nonsense. The blind spot is real in the geometry; whether it is exploitable at any attack magnitude that keeps the model coherent is what the next experiments need to answer.

Roadmap over the next four weeks:

  • Causal attack sweep. Map the λ × layer envelope where PC2/PC3 attacks remain coherent and cross the harm threshold — the question this run leaves open. Prediction: a narrower coherent-attack window on Gemma 2 27B than the paper's Qwen results suggest, due to Gemma's larger residual norm at L*=21 (24,440).
  • Subject coverage. Qwen 3 32B and Gemma 4 31B (thinking ON / OFF) — cross-architecture, cross-generation, cross-reasoning-mode PC1 stability across six pairs.
  • Dataset coverage. Shah-reconstructed jailbreaks (synthetic pairs) as a cross-dataset replication check; report tagged by dataset ∈ {dan, shah_reconstructed}.
  • H2 multi-axis defense. Cap AA + PC2 + PC3 jointly; measure whether closing the geometric blind spot also closes the behavioural-bypass observed here.
  • Capability cost. IFEval, MMLU-Pro, GSM8k, EQ-Bench under each cap configuration — harm-vs-capability Pareto frontier.
  • Statistical rigor. Ordinal logistic LASSO on the 9-category judge labels (paper Appendix D.2.2), per-PC FDR (BH q=0.05), Cohen's d on projection differences (PC2: −1.68 within-capped already in this run).
  • Engine work. SGLang --forward-hooks spike to remove the HF/vLLM backend split that Plan B ships with.

Three bugs found and fixed during this run

Plan B's first attempt produced 100% degenerate token-loop outputs across every capped condition ("th'th, the last, the last…"). Three stacked bugs in the cap pipeline, all corrected before this report's numbers:

  1. Sign convention. Lu et al.'s upstream _apply_cap is a ceiling on a role-positive direction; our locally computed AA was Assistant-positive (cos sim with their published Qwen capping vector = −1.0000). Composing them pushed Assistant activations away from Assistant — anti-defense. Fix: negate cap vectors inside from_config.
  2. Tau calibration. Tau was the 25th percentile of role-rollout projections on +AA, but the cap operator (after the sign flip) reads <h, −AA>, so the comparison sign was wrong: cap never fired. Fix: τ_role = −p75 of the existing +AA calibration (mathematically equivalent to p25 in the role-positive distribution).
  3. Capping at the wrong depth. The original fallback (offset L*+6, width 8) sat at 50–65% depth on a model the paper never published a cap range for. The paper's Qwen and Llama caps both sit at 70–83% depth. Fix: relocate to layers [33, 38] at 71.7–82.6% depth, proportionally matching Qwen.

The full bug analysis — including evidence, alternatives considered, reversibility — is in the decisions log linked under Receipts.
