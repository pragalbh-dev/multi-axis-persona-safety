Multi-Axis Persona Safety

Replicating Lu et al.'s Assistant-Axis activation cap on four open-weight models — and asking whether the residual stream under jailbreak still encodes harm-relevant signal in directions the cap ignores. The signal replicates universally; defense robustness does not — on one model the cap fails outright at zero attack and is adversarially exploited for a +12-point harm-rate increase at near-perfect coherence. Multi-axis capping closes ~12% of that blind spot at 99.8% coherence; capability cost decouples sharply from defense effectiveness across the four models.

Why this work

Lu et al. (arXiv 2601.10387, The Assistant Axis) propose a single-direction activation-level defense for persona jailbreaks. They identify a direction in the residual stream — the Assistant Axis (AA), ≈ PC1 of a 275-role activation PCA — and show that capping the projection of activations along AA reduces harmful-information extraction across several frontier open-weight models.

The open questions this work asks: does the residual stream under jailbreak still encode harm-relevant information in directions orthogonal to the Assistant direction, and does that pattern travel across models, architectures, and reasoning configurations? If the orthogonal signal is real and adversarially exploitable, the single-axis cap has a structural blind spot — it can be defeated by an attacker steering along the directions the cap ignores. If the picture is uniform across models, defense recommendations transfer cleanly; if it is heterogeneous, a deployable defense needs to know which regime it is in.

Pipeline validation

Before reporting findings beyond the paper, four checks confirm this is a faithful instance of Lu et al.'s methodology on each of the four models:

  • PC1 ≈ the Assistant direction replicates on every model tested. Paper's cross-model threshold for PC1/Assistant-direction agreement is 0.71. Per-model values: Gemma 2 27B 0.88, Qwen 3 32B 0.90, Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off 0.82, Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on 0.82. PCs are fit on each model's own role-vector cache; the Assistant direction is computed via the contrast-vector recipe (mean(default_assistant) − mean(role_playing)) per paper §3.1.
  • The cap defense replicates direction on three of four models. The Assistant-direction cap reduces baseline harm rate by 13.0 pp on Gemma 2 27B, 6.2 pp on Qwen 3 32B, and 9.9 pp on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on. The exception — Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off, where the cap slightly increases harm at zero attack — is the headline divergence and is detailed below.
  • Coherence is preserved at the operating point. Across the four models, the Assistant-direction-capped models retain 87–100% coherent (non-nonsense) outputs at the operating threshold, with no model in the catastrophic-coherence-loss regime that would degrade harm-rate measurements.
  • Harm-judge protocol matches the paper verbatim. Nine-category prompt from Appendix D.2.2; binarization rule (enough_info ∪ enough_info_and_follow_perfectly → harm=1) per the paper. Judge model is Qwen 3.6 27B (in place of paper's deepseek-v3 per project budget); validated against GPT-5.5 on a 200-sample stratified subset of the Gemma 2 27B run at 93% binary agreement (paper's reference is 91.6% deepseek-v3-vs-human).

What this study found

Five findings across the four models:
  1. The orthogonal-to-Assistant-direction harm signal replicates across all four models. Adding the leading persona principal components to a logistic LASSO lifts harm-prediction AUC by +0.10 to +0.31 over the Assistant-direction-alone baseline; every 95% BCa CI excludes zero. The blind spot Lu et al.'s single-direction cap leaves behind is a general feature, not a Gemma 2 27B artifact.
  2. The cap defends in three of four models — and outright fails in the fourth. On Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off, the Assistant-direction cap increases harm at zero attack (10.2% → 11.2%) and a single harm-aligned principal-component attack lifts harm to 23.6% at 99.8% coherence — a +12.4 percentage-point adversarial recovery on the headline mitigation. Qwen 3 32B and Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on resist all attacks tested at coherence-preserving steering magnitudes. The number of LASSO-selected components does not predict robustness (reasoning-on selects nine components and defends; reasoning-off selects one and fails).
  3. Extended reasoning reorganizes the safety geometry. With reasoning on, the probe layer where role information is most linearly separable shifts from middle depth (~20%) to the network's final layer (98% depth); the Assistant direction alone becomes a much stronger harm classifier (AUC 0.81 vs 0.58 with reasoning off); and the empirical harm direction becomes substantially aligned with the Assistant direction (cos = +0.56) where it is near-orthogonal in the other three models (cos ∈ [−0.36, +0.13]).
  4. Multi-axis capping partially closes the blind spot on the failure model. Adding harm-aligned PC2 and PC3 caps to the Assistant-direction cap on Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off reduces the PC3-attack harm rate from 23.6% to 22.05% at 99.8% coherence — a 1.57 pp reduction, ~12% of the 13.4 pp adversarial recovery margin. The mechanism is geometric: PC2/PC3 caps fire before the PC3 attack injects at the same layer, so the cap clamps pre-attack activations and the attack-perturbed signal propagates downstream uncapped. Multi-axis capping is real but not transformative; full closure requires breaking the same-layer hook ordering or capping at later layers.
  5. Capability cost decouples sharply from defense effectiveness. The Assistant-direction cap is essentially free on Gemma 2 27B and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off (≤1 pp loss on every bench tested) but catastrophic on Qwen 3 32B (IFEval −41 pp, GSM8k −33 pp) and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on (IFEval −50 pp, EQ-Bench −16 pp). Cost and effectiveness are not aligned: Qwen's cap is fully effective and expensive; reasoning-off's cap is ineffective and free. Multi-axis cap on Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off keeps the loss within ~1.5 pp.

The takeaway: the orthogonal-to-Assistant-direction blind spot is a general feature of role-conditioned residual streams, not a single-model artifact — but defense behaviour around it is heterogeneous in two dimensions at once. Effectiveness varies (one cap-failure model out of four), capability cost varies (50 pp swing on a single benchmark), and the two don't track each other. A deployable recommendation needs to know which regime each target model is in.

What replicates across all four models

The orthogonal harm signal is universal

Across the four models, fitting a logistic LASSO over {Assistant-direction, top-10 persona PCs} at each model's probe layer yields a harm-prediction AUC strictly greater than the Assistant-direction-alone baseline. Lifts range from +0.10 on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on (the smallest gap) to +0.31 on Qwen 3 32B (the largest), with 95% BCa CIs excluding zero on every model. The blind spot Lu et al.'s single-direction cap leaves behind is a general feature of role-conditioned residual streams.

Per-model details: Qwen 3 32B sits at AUC 0.628 → 0.930 (lift +0.312, CI [+0.214, +0.415]; LASSO selects PC3–PC8). Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off sits at 0.576 → 0.866 (lift +0.295, CI [+0.191, +0.406]; LASSO selects PC4 alone). Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on sits at 0.813 → 0.905 (lift +0.101, CI [+0.051, +0.155]; LASSO selects PC2–PC10). Gemma 2 27B from the original study sits at 0.728 → 0.967 (lift +0.240, CI [+0.209, +0.273]; LASSO selects PC2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 10).

The cap defends in three of four models

Capping projections along the Assistant direction at the role-positive 25th percentile reduces baseline harm rate on three of four models — by 13.0 pp on Gemma 2 27B (14.8% → 1.8%), 6.2 pp on Qwen 3 32B (8.6% → 2.4%), and 9.9 pp on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on (15.0% → 5.1%). On the same three models, all harm-aligned principal-component and empirical-harm-direction attacks tested at coherence-preserving steering magnitudes recover at most +1.4 pp above the defended baseline. The fourth model, Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off, is the exception — flagged below.

Where the four models diverge

Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off — the cap fails and is exploitable

Two failures stack on this model. First, applying the Assistant-direction cap at the role-positive 25th percentile pushes binary harm rate from 10.2% to 11.2% — a 1.0 pp increase at zero attack. The defense paper's headline mitigation slightly worsens harm on this model. Second, on the cap-defended model, a single harm-aligned principal-component attack recovers harm decisively: steering along the harm-aligned direction of PC3 at λ=0.25 lifts harm to 23.6% at 99.8% coherence — +12.4 percentage points above the defended baseline, with the model continuing to produce coherent, on-topic, in-distribution responses while being adversarially driven past the defense. A null-space attack at the same λ reaches 18.7% at 99.0% coherence (+7.5 pp).

A non-obvious property of this failure: the LASSO selected only PC4 as a non-zero feature on this model, yet the most successful attack steers along PC3. Predictive feature selection and adversarial exploitability are dissociable here — carried forward as Q6 below.

Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on — different geometry, same conclusion

Same model weights as above, with extended-reasoning mode toggled on. Three things change. First, the probe layer where role information is most linearly separable shifts from layer 14 of 60 (~23% depth) to layer 59 of 60 (98% depth) — effectively the network's final layer.

Second, the Assistant direction alone becomes a much stronger harm classifier on this model — AUC 0.81 vs. 0.58 with reasoning off — and the residual blind spot shrinks correspondingly (lift +0.10 vs +0.30 with reasoning off). Third, the empirical harm direction (the difference of mean activations between harm-positive and harm-negative prompts at the probe layer) becomes substantially aligned with the Assistant direction (cos = +0.56), where it is near-orthogonal in the other three models.

The defense behaves accordingly — the Assistant-direction cap is robust on this model (−9.9 pp at zero attack; the strongest harm-aligned PC2 attack at λ=0.25 recovers only +1.4 pp). The framing: extended reasoning concentrates the safety-relevant geometry into the Assistant direction, the residual blind spot shrinks, and the cap that holds elsewhere holds here too.

Qwen 3 32B — most robust defense observed

Despite carrying the largest blind-spot AUC lift among the four models (+0.31), all four attack classes — harm-aligned PC2, harm-aligned PC3, the empirical harm direction, and a null-space attack — fail to recover any harm above the cap-defended baseline at λ ∈ [0.15, 0.25]. Coherence stays above 97% on every harm-aligned attack and above 90% on the null-space attack. The framing: the cap is geometrically near-complete on this model at the steering magnitudes tested. Whether stronger attacks at higher λ would succeed before coherence collapse is open.

Multi-axis defense — partial closure on the failure model

The orthogonal blind spot Lu et al.'s single-direction cap leaves behind is, in principle, exactly the gap a multi-axis cap should close: cap the Assistant direction plus the leading harm-aligned persona PCs jointly, calibrate per-axis τ against role projections, and the geometrically-aware defense should clamp the same activations the PC attack relies on. We test this directly on the failure model — Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off — on a 508-prompt held-out test split.

Per-axis τ is calibrated on a 200-prompt validation subset by sweeping τ ∈ {p1, p10, p25, p50, p75} of each axis's role-positive projection distribution at the probe layer L*=14. The chosen pair (τ_PC2 = p10, τ_PC3 = p25) maximises harm reduction under the strongest available attack (harm-aligned PC3 at λ=0.25) subject to a 90% coherence floor. A first attempt that re-used PC2/PC3 as cap directions across all eight Assistant-cap layers (27–34) collapsed coherence to 0% at every τ tested — the per-layer projection magnitudes were 80–180 units larger than the validation distribution implied, and stacking eight clipped layers nuked the residual stream. The corrected design caps each persona PC at its native extraction layer (L*=14) only, leaving the Assistant cap unchanged across the full 27–34 range.

Why the closure isn't larger. At the same probe layer, the cap fires before the attack injection in the forward pass — the steering library registers cap hooks, then steer hooks, and PyTorch fires forward hooks in registration order. So at L*=14: the cap clamps pre-attack activations, the attack adds a harm-positive perturbation immediately afterwards, and the perturbed signal propagates through L=15..26 uncapped. The Assistant-direction cap at layers 27–34 catches some residual harm but cannot fully recover. Closing the gap entirely would require either (a) reversing hook order so the cap fires after the attack at the same layer, or (b) propagating the L*=14 PC2/PC3 cap directions forward to deeper layers — the latter is what broke coherence in the first calibration attempt. Deployment-grade multi-axis defense looks tractable but needs per-layer cap-direction refit.

Why we ran this only on the failure model. Multi-axis cap is the right experiment specifically when single-axis cap leaves a measurable adversarial gap. Qwen 3 32B's AA-cap is already saturating (no harm-aligned attack at any tested λ recovers more than 1.4 pp); Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on similarly. Multi-axis cap calibration on those two would either be redundant or could collapse coherence the way it did on reasoning-off's first attempt — extending the multi-axis defense to the other three subjects is left for follow-up work, with calibration logic adjusted per the lesson here (single-layer cap at native L*, τ per axis from its own role distribution).

Capability cost — AA-cap is not free everywhere

The harm reductions reported above are only half the picture. A defense that reduces harm by 9 pp but kills 50 pp of instruction-following is not deployable. We measure the capability cost on three benchmarks — IFEval (instruction following, n=541), GSM8k (math, n=1000), EQ-Bench (emotional-intelligence, n=171) — under unsteered, Assistant-direction-cap, and (on the failure model) multi-axis-cap conditions.

The cost-effectiveness map across the four models:

  • Gemma 2 27B — cap is free and effective. AA-cap loss is ≤0.4 pp on every benchmark (IFEval 84.9 → 84.8, GSM8k 84.3 → 83.9, EQ-Bench 87.1 → 87.2). The cap also reduces baseline harm by 13.0 pp. This is the regime Lu et al.'s single-axis recommendation was validated against.
  • Qwen 3 32B — cap is effective but catastrophically expensive. IFEval 90.1 → 49.2 (−41 pp), GSM8k 91.5 → 58.4 (−33 pp), EQ-Bench 86.9 → 86.1 (−0.8 pp). The cap fully blocks every harm-aligned attack we tested, but at the price of breaking instruction-following and arithmetic reasoning. Whether a less-aggressive τ would preserve more capability while still defending is open.
  • Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off — cap is free but ineffective. AA-cap loss is ≤0.8 pp across all benches (IFEval 94.1 → 93.3, GSM8k 90.4 → 89.9, EQ-Bench 88.7 → 88.8). The multi-axis-cap (AA + PC2 + PC3) keeps the loss within 1.5 pp on each bench (IFEval 93.4, GSM8k 88.9, EQ-Bench 88.7). This is good news for the multi-axis closure result above — the capability cost of partially closing the blind spot is essentially zero.
  • Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on — cap is effective but devastating to reasoning. IFEval 93.7 → 44.2 (−50 pp), EQ-Bench 88.7 → 72.8 (−16 pp). GSM8k 90.6 → 90.0 (−0.6 pp) is the surprising exception — arithmetic reasoning survives. The IFEval collapse is the largest single capability-cost number in the four-model panel. Whether reasoning-mode-specific cap calibration (lower τ, fewer cap layers, or a different layer range entirely) would restore IFEval is open.

The decoupling. Effectiveness and cost are not aligned across the four models. Qwen and reasoning-on have effective caps with high cost; reasoning-off has a free cap with no effect; Gemma 2 27B is the unique "free and effective" combination. A deployment recommendation that reads "apply the Assistant-direction cap" treats four very different operating points as one. The failure mode is not "the cap doesn't work on every model" — it is "the cap's tradeoff curve is heterogeneous and the per-model regime needs measurement before deployment."

MMLU-Pro was dropped from this evaluation per project budget; multi-axis-cap capability rows for Qwen 3 32B and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on are absent because the multi-axis defense was scoped to the failure model only (see preceding section). Both gaps are pickup work and will be done later if a reviewer asks for the symmetric numbers.

Causal vs predictive — clean v_harm steering on baseline prompts

The cross-model AUC lift in Figure A is a predictive finding: per-prompt activations carry harm-relevant signal in directions orthogonal to the Assistant direction. The natural question is whether that signal is also causal — if we steer the residual stream along the empirical harm direction v_harm = mean(harm_activations) − mean(safe_activations) on clean baseline prompts, with no cap and no jailbreak attack, does harm rate go up?

We run the test on the two Gemma 4 31B subjects (the geometries where v_harm is most informative): reasoning-off has cos(v_harm, AA) = 0.05, the maximally-orthogonal case across the four subjects; reasoning-on has cos(v_harm, AA) = 0.56, the only subject where v_harm overlaps the Assistant direction substantially. Steering coefficient λ ∈ {0.10, 0.25, 0.50}, n=508 baseline DAN prompts per cell, no cap, no attack vector other than v_harm itself.

Reasoning-off — weak causal effect, well below the threshold. At λ=0.10 (full coherence), harm lifts by +4.2 pp (10.2% → 14.4%) with the coherence-conditioned 95% CI [+1.0, +7.3] excluding zero. The lift is real but does not clear the +5 pp pre-registered threshold. At λ=0.25 the lift drops to +2.6 pp and the CI touches zero — the response is non-monotonic in λ, suggesting early coherence drift starting to suppress signal even at 95% coherence. At λ=0.50 the model collapses (coherence 0.2%, only 1 of 508 responses survives classification); the gross harm rate of 0% is therefore uninformative about causality, not evidence against it.

Reasoning-on — v_harm is anti-causal at all coherent λ. Gross and coherence-conditioned harm both drop by ~11 pp (15.0% → ~4%) at every λ tested, with coherence holding at 99.6% across the sweep. The mechanism is geometric: cos(v_harm, PC1) = 0.78 with PC1 ≈ Assistant-direction at this subject's probe layer (L*=59), so adding v_harm steers the residual stream toward the Assistant pole rather than away from it. The behavioural surface, however, isn't “more refusals” (refusal rate slightly drops, 68.8% → 63.6%) but off-topic deflection — out_of_context labels jump from 3.2% baseline to 18.9% at λ=0.10. The model stays grammatical and on-task in form while disengaging from the harmful question.

What this tells us about the +12.4 pp blind spot. The Phase B headline (PC3 attack on Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off lifts harm by +12.4 pp under the Assistant-direction cap) cannot be reproduced by clean v_harm steering — the largest coherent v_harm lift is +4.2 pp, well short of the under-cap recovery. The implication is that PC3's adversarial value is a cap-failure mode, not a residual-stream harm direction we can simply steer along: PC3 attack works specifically because the Assistant-cap fires before it at the probe layer (the same hook-ordering mechanism that limited multi-axis closure to ~12% above), not because PC3 contains "more harm" than other directions in some abstract sense. v_harm steering on its own runs into the model's coherence ceiling well before producing comparable harm.

A finer λ sweep through {0.30, 0.35, 0.40, 0.45} on reasoning-off would map the coherence-decay window where v_harm "wants to" produce harm but hasn't yet collapsed the model. We did not run it — the existing data is sufficient to support the qualitative claim above (clean v_harm has weak causal effect; the under-cap PC3 attack is the harder-to-defend pathway). A reader who wants the threshold-call settled either way should treat that fine sweep as the natural follow-up.

Comparator: Gemma 2 27B under cap. The follow-up section below shows that AA-cap + v_harm steering at λ=0.10 on Gemma 2 27B recovers harm to 14.6% at 76.7% coherence (19.0% among coherent responses) — an unambiguous causal lift in the coherent regime. But on Gemma 2 27B, cos(v_harm, PC2) = −0.71: v_harm is essentially anti-PC2, and PC2-attack-under-cap on the same model recovers 11.1% harm at 91% coherence. The G2 v_harm result is therefore PC2-attack in disguise, not evidence of a separable causal harm direction. The cross-model picture is consistent: v_harm is parallel to whichever role-PC dominates the harm-vs-safe contrast at the probe layer; when that PC happens to be one the cap fails to clamp (G2 PC2, g4_off PC3), the under-cap attack is causally exploitable; when it doesn't (Qwen, g4_on), the cap holds.

The defense and what it leaves behind

Behavioural bypass: refusal-style erasure under PC attack

Under AA-cap alone, the model refuses jailbreak prompts in an explicit Assistant register — "I cannot", "I'm sorry", "not appropriate", and similar phrases (a fixed regex of explicit-refusal patterns). Under PC2 and PC3 attacks at the same λ that left random attacks producing only nonsense, the explicit refusal register collapses while the model remains coherent.

Geometry of harm: v_harm decomposition

Direct test of Q1 from the next-phase plan. Computed v_harm = mean(harm_activations) − mean(safe_activations) on the merged DAN+Shah baseline (n=2,207, 243 harm-positive) at L*=21, then decomposed onto the role-PCA basis.

Sign-corrected per-axis attack arm

The earlier attacks at λ=+0.25 along PC2/PC3 hit 67% / 44% nonsense. The decomposition above explains why: cos_sim(v_harm, PC2) = −0.64, so positive-λ PC2 steering pointed toward safety, not toward harm. The model was being pushed past Assistant-mode into off-distribution non-Assistant non-harmful states — coherent attacks were testing the wrong direction.

The corrected per-axis attack steers along sign(cos(v_harm, PC_i)) · PC_i — toward harm under our extraction's sign convention. Adaptive λ sweep (start 0.05, raise until coherence < 80%) on a stratified subset (243 harm-positive + 243 controls):

The H1 blind-spot is real and adversarial. Sign-corrected PC2 and PC3 attacks recover harm rate from 1.8% (AA-cap baseline) to 11–12% at coherence-preserving λ — a 6× lift, on directions AA-cap doesn't defend. v_harm-direct is the strongest single-direction attack at 14.6%, validating it as the upper bound on what any linear direction can achieve. (This resolves Q2: the predictive direction causes harm, not just correlates with it.)

Two surprises. (i) PC4 alone strips refusal style without producing harm. At λ=0.15 with 78% coherence, PC4-attack drops the refusal-keyword rate to ~5% but its harm rate is only 1%. The model finds non-harmful ways to deflect when the refusal voice is suppressed by a low-cos-with-v_harm axis. (ii) The multi-axis composite is dominated by PC4 (its λ=0.15 is 3× the PC2/PC3 components), so the composite inherits PC4's "refusal collapse without harm production" pattern. Multi-axis attack does not stack constructively when the per-axis λ's differ this much; future work would equalize axis weights or use the LASSO-derived weighting directly.

Multi-axis defense (interaction matrix)

Direct test of Q3. Three multi-axis cap configurations were composed with the strongest Phase 3 attacks on the harm-positive subset (n=243 per cell). Each axis capped at p25 of its role-positive distribution.

Three findings, in increasing order of relevance to a deployable defense:

  1. Stacking 4 caps at p25 each is catastrophically over-aggressive. aa_pc2_pc3_pc4 produces 100% nonsense even with zero attack. The harm rate is “0%” only because there is no coherent text to be judged. This is the over-cap regime: tau calibration that works in isolation does not compose — each additional cap compounds the activation clipping. Deployable multi-axis defense requires per-axis Pareto-optimized tau, not p25 everywhere.
  2. The v_harm-direct cap also degrades coherence at p25 alone. aa_v_harm with zero attack hits 40% nonsense — v_harm has different magnitude statistics than AA, so its p25 tau translates to a more aggressive intervention. Per-axis tau is not transferable; v_harm's tau needs its own grid search.
  3. Single-extra-axis cap (aa_pc2) preserves coherence (91%) but its H2 win is marginal. Under matched PC2 attack: refusal stays at 45% (vs 51% baseline; closer to no-attack than the AA-only-cap's 12 pp drop under same attack), but binary harm rate is 21.0% — comparable to the AA-only-cap-on-harm-positive value. The PC2 cap closes the behavioural channel (refusal style) but not the harm-rate channel.

Refined H2 verdict. Multi-axis defense is not a free lunch. AA-cap was already absorbing the bulk of linearly-available harm-relevant variance — consistent with v_harm having only 13% energy outside the top-AA-aligned directions. Adding a single extra axis closes the refusal-style channel under a matched attack but does not compound to a stronger harm-defense; adding three extra axes at the same percentile breaks coherence entirely. The blind spot is real (Phase 3 confirmed it adversarially) but a defense that closes it without coherence loss requires per-axis Pareto tau calibration that is left for next-phase work.

Limitations

  • Cross-model coverage is partial. Four models, three of them Gemma-family. The cross-architecture claim rests on Qwen 3 32B alone; broader architecture coverage (e.g., Llama, Mistral, DBRX) is open.
  • One attack distribution. DAN persona-jailbreak prompts are the attack class for all four models. Cross-dataset replication on Shah-reconstructed synthetic jailbreaks (Shah et al. 2311.03348 methodology) is currently Gemma 2 27B only.
  • Capability evaluation is partial. Three benches reported (IFEval, GSM8k, EQ-Bench) across all four models; MMLU-Pro is dropped per project budget and revivable on demand. Multi-axis-cap capability rows for Qwen 3 32B and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on are absent because the multi-axis defense is scoped to the failure model only — both gaps are pickup work and will be done if a reviewer asks for the symmetric numbers.
  • Ext B fine λ sweep deferred. The clean v_harm causal test on Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off used λ ∈ {0.10, 0.25, 0.50}. The intermediate window λ ∈ {0.30, 0.35, 0.40, 0.45} — where coherence is decaying but not yet collapsed and v_harm might still be steering toward harm — was not probed. The +4.2 pp lift at λ=0.10 (CI excludes zero, below the +5 pp threshold) is therefore conservative on the upper bound; whether the lift would clear the threshold inside the coherence-decay window is open.
  • Reasoning-mode comparison is within a single model. The geometry-reorganization-with-reasoning result (probe layer at 98% depth; harm direction substantially aligned with the Assistant direction) is on Gemma 4 31B only. Whether this generalizes to other reasoning-capable models is open.
  • Probe-layer-at-final-layer caveat. The probe layer at 98% depth on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on sits at the network's penultimate layer. Whether this is a genuine final-layer phenomenon or an artifact of the role-separation argmax criterion (which favours later layers when role information accumulates) is open.
  • The follow-up extension findings are Gemma 2 27B only. Cross-dataset replication on Shah jailbreaks, the empirical-harm-direction decomposition, the sign-corrected per-axis attack arm, and the multi-axis defense interaction matrix are all currently single-model. Replication on the three new models — particularly the multi-axis defense interaction matrix — is the natural next step.
  • Random-direction baselines at λ=0.25 collapse model coherence on Gemma 2 27B. The PC-vs-random contrast at this λ is therefore degenerate on that model; the cleanest version of the random-baseline claim requires a coherence-matched random-direction sweep at lower λ. On the three new models, the per-axis attack protocol uses harm-aligned-and-sign-corrected directions with coherence-preserving λ values (0.15–0.25), which side-steps this issue.
  • LASSO is predictive; causal exploitability is dissociable. Causal exploit demonstrated on two of four models (Gemma 2 27B with empirical-harm-direction-direct attack at λ=0.10, and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off with harm-aligned PC3 attack at λ=0.25). The other two models resist all harm-aligned attacks tested at coherence-preserving steering magnitudes — whether stronger attacks at higher λ would succeed before coherence collapse is open. Note also that LASSO selection and adversarial exploitability are not the same on Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off (LASSO picks PC4; the most successful attack steers along PC3).
  • Within-cap Cohen's d (Gemma 2 27B only). Only n=9 of the 500 capped responses on Gemma 2 27B are judged enough_info, which gives the within-cap harm vs safe Cohen's d a small positive group. The CI is reported but the underlying group size limits how tight the bound can be made.
  • Cross-judge agreement validation is Gemma 2 27B only. Primary judge (Qwen 3.6 27B) is validated against GPT-5.5 at 93% binary agreement on a 200-sample stratified subset of the Gemma 2 27B run (paper reports 91.6% binary agreement against human labels for deepseek-v3). Re-validation per-model is not yet performed.

Research questions for the next phase

The single-axis result raised five questions; the cross-model extension to three additional models added two more. Resolutions are noted in-place; carry-forwards are flagged.

  1. Is the harm signal Assistant-direction-aligned or genuinely orthogonal? RESOLVED with nuance. Orthogonal in three of four models — cos(empirical harm direction, Assistant direction) is −0.36 on Gemma 2 27B, +0.13 on Qwen 3 32B, and +0.05 on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off. The exception is Gemma 4 31B with reasoning on, where the harm direction becomes substantially aligned with the Assistant direction (cos = +0.56). Extended reasoning reorganizes the geometry; the original H1 framing holds in the non-reasoning regime.
  2. Do the harm-predictive directions cause harm, or merely correlate with it? RESOLVED with nuance. Three findings, in order of strength: (a) Harm-aligned PC3 steering on Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off, applied under the Assistant-direction cap at λ=0.25, lifts harm to 23.6% at 99.8% coherence (+12.4 pp above the defended baseline) — an unambiguous causal exploit of a cap-failure mode. (b) Empirical-harm-direction (v_harm) steering on Gemma 2 27B under cap at λ=0.10 lifts harm to 14.6% (19.0% among coherent responses) — also causal in the coherent regime, but the geometric reason is cos(v_harm, PC2) = −0.71 on this subject: v_harm is anti-PC2, and PC2-attack-under-cap recovers harm independently. The G2 v_harm result is PC2-attack in disguise, not evidence of a separable causal harm direction. (c) Clean v_harm steering (no cap, no jailbreak attack) at coherence-preserving λ on the two Gemma 4 31B subjects produces only +4.2 pp (reasoning-off, CI [+1.0, +7.3] excludes zero but below the +5 pp threshold) or anti-causal suppression (reasoning-on, −11 pp at all λ). The implication: the orthogonal blind spot is causally exploitable specifically as a cap-failure mode, not as a residual-stream harm direction one can simply steer along on undefended models. See the Causal vs predictive section above for the full coherence-conditioned read.
  3. Does capping the Assistant direction plus higher persona PCs jointly close the orthogonal signal? RESOLVED on the failure model with partial closure (~12%); follow-up needed for full closure. On Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off, AA + PC2 + PC3 cap at calibrated per-axis τ (PC2 at p10, PC3 at p25, native L*=14 only) reduces the PC3-attack harm rate from 23.6% to 22.05% at 99.8% coherence — a 1.57 pp / ~12% closure of the 13.4 pp adversarial recovery margin. PC2 cap alone has zero or slightly negative effect; the PC3 cap carries the closure. Mechanism: the cap fires before the attack at the same probe layer, so the cap clamps pre-attack activations and the attack-perturbed signal propagates downstream uncapped. Full closure would require either reversing hook order at the same layer or refitting per-layer cap directions for L = 15..34 — both are tractable follow-ups. Multi-axis cap on Gemma 2 27B follow-up (single-extra-axis caps under the multi-axis-attack interaction matrix) shows the AA+PC2 combination preserves coherence at 91% and stabilizes refusal style but does not reduce binary harm rate, while stacking three extra axes at p25 each collapses coherence entirely — consistent with the L*-only single-layer rule used on the failure model. Multi-axis cap on Qwen 3 32B and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on are absent — pickup work, will be done later.
  4. What do the higher persona PCs actually do to model voice when they preserve coherence? Carries forward. Mechanistic interpretation of which persona dimensions PC2/PC3/etc. govern (Chen et al.-style persona-vectors thread) remains open. Particularly relevant given that on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off, the LASSO selects PC4 but the strongest adversarial attack steers along PC3.
  5. Does the blind-spot signal replicate cross-architecture and cross-dataset? RESOLVED on cross-architecture; cross-dataset partial. The orthogonal-to-Assistant-direction harm signal lifts AUC by +0.10 to +0.31 on every model tested, with 95% CIs excluding zero. Defense robustness is heterogeneous — including the frontier-model failure mode on Gemma 4 31B with reasoning off. Cross-dataset replication on Shah-reconstructed jailbreaks remains Gemma 2 27B only.
  6. What predicts defense robustness across models? NEW — carries forward. The number of LASSO-selected components is not predictive: reasoning-on selects nine and defends; reasoning-off selects one and fails. LASSO selection and adversarial exploitability are also dissociable on reasoning-off (LASSO picks PC4; the strongest attack steers along PC3). Candidate predictors to evaluate as more models come online: cos(empirical harm direction, Assistant direction), the Assistant-direction-only AUC, and the per-model probe-layer depth as a fraction of network depth.
  7. What does the harm-vs-capability Pareto look like across the four models? RESOLVED across IFEval / GSM8k / EQ-Bench. Cost decouples sharply from effectiveness: AA-cap is essentially free on Gemma 2 27B (≤0.4 pp loss on every bench) and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-off (≤1 pp), but catastrophic on Qwen 3 32B (IFEval −41 pp, GSM8k −33 pp) and Gemma 4 31B reasoning-on (IFEval −50 pp, EQ-Bench −16 pp). Multi-axis cap on the failure model adds at most 1.5 pp on top of the (already negligible) AA-cap loss. The deployable-defense regime is the (effective AND cheap) intersection — only Gemma 2 27B occupies it cleanly out of the four models. Full table in the Capability cost section above. MMLU-Pro is dropped from this evaluation per project budget; multi-axis-cap rows for Qwen and reasoning-on are absent (defense is scoped to the failure model only) and are pickup work for later.
